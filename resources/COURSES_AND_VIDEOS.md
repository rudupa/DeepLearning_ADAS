# 🎓 Free Courses & Video Resources

Curated free courses and video series for learning deep learning and ADAS.

---

## 🏆 Top Recommended Courses (Start Here)

### 1. Stanford CS231n — Convolutional Neural Networks for Visual Recognition
- **URL:** https://cs231n.stanford.edu/
- **Videos:** YouTube (Spring 2017 lectures by Andrej Karpathy)
- **Level:** Intermediate
- **Duration:** ~20 hours
- **Why:** Gold standard for learning CNNs and computer vision. Taught by pioneers in the field.
- **Topics:** Image classification, detection, segmentation, RNNs, GANs

### 2. Stanford CS224n — Natural Language Processing with Deep Learning
- **URL:** https://web.stanford.edu/class/cs224n/
- **Videos:** YouTube
- **Level:** Intermediate
- **Duration:** ~20 hours
- **Why:** Best course for understanding transformers, attention, and language models.
- **Topics:** Word embeddings, RNNs, Transformers, BERT, GPT, NLG

### 3. Fast.ai — Practical Deep Learning for Coders
- **URL:** https://course.fast.ai/
- **Videos:** YouTube
- **Level:** Beginner
- **Duration:** ~30 hours
- **Why:** Top-down approach — build real projects first, understand theory later.
- **Topics:** Image classification, NLP, tabular data, collaborative filtering

### 4. MIT 6.S191 — Introduction to Deep Learning
- **URL:** http://introtodeeplearning.com/
- **Videos:** YouTube
- **Level:** Beginner
- **Duration:** ~10 hours
- **Why:** Concise, well-produced. Great for a quick overview of the entire field.
- **Topics:** Neural networks, CNNs, RNNs, generative models, RL

## 🤗 Transformers & LLMs

### 8. Andrej Karpathy — Neural Networks: Zero to Hero
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLAqhIrjkxbuWI23v9cThsA9GvCAUhRvKZ
- **Level:** Beginner to Intermediate
- **Duration:** ~15 hours
- **Why:** Build GPT from scratch! One of the best learning experiences available.
- **Videos:** micrograd, makemore, nanoGPT

### 9. Hugging Face Course
- **URL:** https://huggingface.co/learn
- **Level:** Beginner to Intermediate
- **Duration:** ~20 hours
- **Why:** Learn to use the transformers library effectively.
- **Topics:** NLP, multimodal models, fine-tuning, deployment

### 10. Stanford CS25 — Transformers United
- **URL:** https://web.stanford.edu/class/cs25/
- **Videos:** YouTube
- **Level:** Advanced
- **Duration:** ~15 hours
- **Why:** Cutting-edge transformer research, guest lectures from top researchers.

## 🎯 Recommended Learning Path

```
Week 1-2:  3Blue1Brown NN series → MIT 6.S191
Week 3-6:  CS231n (CNNs, detection)
Week 7-9:  Karpathy's Zero to Hero (transformers, GPT)
Week 10-12: CS224n (NLP, LLMs)
Week 13-14: HuggingFace Course (VLMs, practical)
Week 15+:  MIT Self-Driving Cars + Build projects!
```
